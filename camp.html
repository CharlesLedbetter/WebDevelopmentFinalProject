Session 1

Drone Racers

In Session 1, "Drone Racers", the campers build there own remote controlled flying drone racers. Each camper is provided with a kit that has everything they will need to design and build a drone of there very own that they can take home with them at the close of the camp session. This camp session emphasizes individuality and good sportsmanship. The campers will build their drones individually, though they are encouraged to work in groups to share ideas and solve technical issues. At the end of the camp session a racing tournament is held divided by age group. Those who win are invited to attend the annual Drone Grand Prix all expenses payed with VIP passes.

Session 1

More Details

Session 1 is divided into four age groups. 8-10, 11-13, 14-15, and 16-17. The campers only need to bring clothing and toiletries, but cell phones and computers are welcome. We have a special GPS tag system to track any laptops the student brings to camp to ensure that no expensive items go missing.


The following is a list of activities available to campers:

    Opening Ceremony
  • Camp Orientation
  • Daily Meals and Entertainment (3 a day)
  • Creativity Sessions
  • 'Ask an Engineer' Sessions
  • Hands-On Drone Building Sessions
  • Racing Practice Sessions
  • Socializing Activities
  • Drone Racing Tournament
  • Family Day
  • Closing Ceremony

Session 2

Robot Puzzlers

In Session 2, "Robot Puzzlers", the campers build from scratch robots that can solve complex puzzles. Each camper joins a team of campers that work together to build the robot. All materials and design software are provided. All the camper needs is their imagination. This camp session emphasizes team work and cognitive puzzle solving. At the end of the session a competition is held divided by age group. Each team's robot works to solve the puzzle their age group was given. Those who win are invited to attend the annual Drone Grand Prix all expenses payed with VIP passes.

Session 3

Robot Warriors

In Session 3, "Robot Warriors", the campers build from scratch bipedal robots that will battle each other in the arena. Each camper joins a team of campers that work together to build the robot. All materials and design software are provided. All the camper needs is their imagination and a competitive spirit. This camp session emphasizes team work, competitive design, and good sportsmanship. At the end of the session a competition is held divided by age group. Each team's robot warrior will face off in a tournament. Those who win are invited to attend TocMax Robotics' International RoboBattle Royal all expenses payed with VIP passes.
